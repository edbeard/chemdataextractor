Interaction of aqueous sulfonephthalein dyes , viz. , phenol red, cresol red and cresol purple with alkyltrimethylammonium and alkylpyridinium halide surfactants in submicellar concentration ranges of the surfactants has been studied by UVâ€“VIS spectroscopy . The spectral changes of the dyes observed on addition of the surfactants at concentrations far below critical micelle concentration (c.m.c.) indicate the formation of doubly deprotonated dye anions, which otherwise are formed only at much higher pH in the absence of the surfactant . The equilibrium constants for the interactions were determined. The deprotonation has been found to increase considerably with increase in hydrophobicity of the surfactant . It has been suggested that combined electrostaticâ€“hydrophobic interaction increases acidity of the sulfonephthalein dyes in their closely packed dye â€“ surfactant ion-pairs, formed at concentrations of surfactant far below c.m.c., causing deprotonation of the dye .

Introduction

Combined electrostatic and hydrophobic interactions pertain to biologically important processes. 1,2 Information on interactions between dyes and ionic surfactants can add to our understanding of combined electrostaticâ€“hydrophobic interactions. 3,4 Variations in the spectral behaviour of aqueous ionic dyes , observed on addition of oppositely charged ionic surfactants below their critical micelle concentration (c.m.c.) have been attributed to formation of dyeâ€“ surfactant salt, ion-pair, molecular complex, dye -rich induced micelles, induced self-aggregates of dye , aggregation of dyeâ€“surfactant complex and change in chromophore microenvironment. 5â€“8 However, the nature of interaction may vary with different types of dyeâ€“surfactant systems. Formation of insoluble or poorly soluble salts or ion-pairs between oppositely charged dye and surfactant at submicellar concentration of the surfactant are well-known phenomena. 8â€“10 The interaction products formed in some phenazinium dye â€“anionic surfactant systems, however, are reported to be soluble enough to permit thermodynamic study of the interaction. 11,12 Three sulfonephthalein dyes , viz. , phenol red (PR), cresol red (CR) and cresol purple (CP) ( Scheme 1 ) have also been found to show spectral variations on addition of cationic surfactants in submicellar concentration ranges without giving precipitation of insoluble salts. 13 In this paper we report a novel observation of deprotonation of these dyes in dye â€“ surfactant ion-pair in such systems due to combined electrostaticâ€“hydrophobic interaction at surfactant concentrations far below the c.m.c.

The DH âˆ’ form ( Î» max 431 nm) of the dye exists in the pure aqueous solution. On addition of CTAB up to 8.0 Ã— 10 âˆ’4 mol dm âˆ’3 the pH of the dye solutions gradually decreased from 5.44 to 4.90. Similar pH variations of aqueous dye solution on addition of cationic surfactants were observed earlier in case of another dye . 19 The Î» max (572 nm) of the absorption band of the dye , in presence of the surfactant below c.m.c., is exactly same as the absorption band of the D 2âˆ’ form in micellar CTAB solution under basic conditions ( Fig. 1 ). 13 The shift of the band from 559 nm in pure water in basic condition to 572 nm in the presence of micellar alkyltrimethylammonium bromide surfactants under basic conditions has been attributed to change of microenvironment of the dye solubilized under micelle in basic conditions. 8,19 Therefore, the 572 nm band of PR observed in the presence of CTAB below c.m.c. may also be attributed to the D 2âˆ’ form of the dye .

It is interesting to note that the 572 nm band of PR initially increased with increasing concentration of the surfactant up to its c.m.c. in the presence of the dye but starts decreasing gradually as more surfactant is added above the c.m.c. Now, a question to be answered here isâ€”how does this 572 nm band, which decreases on appearance of micelles, arise? Any type of involvement of micelles in the appearance of the 572 nm band at far below c.m.c. can be ruled out since the band again starts decreasing as the concentration of the surfactant exceeds the c.m.c. Had micelles been involved in the appearance of the band, the intensity of the band would have increased continuously with increasing micelle concentration above c.m.c.

Anionic and nonionic surfactants have no effect on PR below c.m.c., although they increase their p K a2 above c.m.c. 17,20 This indicates the primary requirement of electrostatic attraction between the dye and the surfactant for the interactions giving rise to the band at 572 nm. We reported earlier that aqueous phenazinium dyes form closely packed hydrophobic ion-pairs 21 with sodium dodecyl sulfate (SDS) in submicellar solutions where the dye undergoes protonation due to combined electrostaticâ€“hydrophobic interaction without addition of any acid. 11 Evidence of hydrophobicity-induced protonation of dye has been observed also in the ion-pair between neutral red and SDS in aqueous solution of pH 7 of phosphate buffer . 22 The observed decrease in the pH of PR solution from 5.44 to 4.90 on addition of CTAB up to 8.0 Ã— 10 âˆ’4 mol dm âˆ’3 , in the present case, is indicative of deprotonation of the dye . It is possible that due to strong hydrophobicity the oppositely charged dye and surfactant ions form a very closely packed ion-pair, localising the original negative charge of the dye anion, paving way for a second deprotonation of the dye . The ion-pairs break down as micelles are formed with increasing concentration of surfactant above c.m.c. Solubilization of the dye by micelle probably wins in a competition with ion-pair formation and subsequent deprotonation of the dye . Thus, one can propose that submicellar CTAB forms similar ion-pairs with anionic DH âˆ’ to give S + Â·DH âˆ’ , which subsequently is deprotonated into the form S + Â·D 2âˆ’ ( Scheme 1 ). This type of protonation or deprotonation of the dyes seems possible in view of the reported unusual nature of such ion-pairs. 21

The interaction in PR-CTAB system was also studied at buffered pH of 4.89 and 7.00. At pH 4.89, the equilibrium constant has been found to be smaller than that in pure water . This lower value can be attributed to modification of the water structure by the buffer components. At pH 7.00, the absorbances of the 559 nm band started increasing continuously on addition of CTAB up to well above c.m.c. with gradual shifting of the Î» max to 572 nm. Conclusions

The present study reveals that cationic surfactants at submicellar concentrations can deprotonate the sulfonephthalein dyes , viz. , PR, CR and CP from their monoanionic forms to dianionic forms in aqueous solutions without any favourable change in pH. It has been suggested that, due to strong hydrophobicity, the oppositely charged dye and surfactant ions form a very closely packed ion-pair, localising the original negative charge of the dye anion, paving the way for a second deprotonation of the dye . Information on such an interaction may add to our understanding of the combined electrostaticâ€“hydrophobic interaction, pertaining to biological systems.
