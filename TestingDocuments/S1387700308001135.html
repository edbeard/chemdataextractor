Synthesis, photophysical, and electrochemical properties of three novel Ru(II) polypyridyl complexes containing dibenzo-18-crown-6

  • Feixiang Cheng,
  • Yang Sun,
  • Weina Wu,
  • Ning Tang ,
  • College of Chemistry and Chemical Engineering, State Key Laboratory of Applied Organic Chemistry, Lanzhou University, Lanzhou 730000, China
Received 28 February 2008, Accepted 11 March 2008, Available online 15 March 2008

Abstract

Three polypyridyl bridging ligands cis -di(4,5-diazafluoren-9-ylimino)dibenzo-18-crown-6 (BL 1 ), trans -di(4,5-diazafluoren-9-ylimino)dibenzo-18-crown-6 (BL 2 ), and 1,4,7,10,13,16-hexaoxacycloctadecyl[2,3:11,12]bis-dipyrido[3,2- a :2′3′- c ]phenazine (BL 3 ), and corresponding dinuclear Ru(II) complexes [(bpy) 2 RuBL 1−3 Ru(bpy) 2 ](PF 6 ) 4 ( 1 , 2 , and 3 ) have been synthesized. Cyclic voltammetry of the complexes are consistent with one Ru(II)-centered oxidation and three ligand-centered reductions. Photophysical behaviors are investigated by UV–Vis absorption and fluorescence spectrometry. The three complexes show metal-to-ligand charge transfer absorption at 445–453 nm and emission at 571–593 nm.

Graphical abstract

Three novel bridging ligands and corresponding dinuclear Ru(II) complexes have been synthesized. These complexes exhibit abundant electrochemical properties, show characteristic MLCT absorption band at 445–453 nm and emission at 571–593 nm.

Ligands containing 2,2′-bipyridine or 1,10-phenanthroline units can in principle be used as precursors for helical assembly, molecular recognition, luminescent devices and so on [1] . Particular interest has been devoted to the design and synthesis of such ligands with a macrocyclic unit attached to a metal polypyridyl core, which have potential application in the preparation of chemosensors. Such chemosensors have drawn increasing attention for their use in fields as diverse as biology, medical analysis, environmental monitoring and so on [2] . Among kinds of metal polypyridyl complexes, Ru(II) polypyridyl complexes have proved to be particularly versatile in sensor applications, because their outstanding photochemical and photophysical properties are quite sensitive to variations of external stimuli [3] . Herein, we report the synthesis and characterization of three ligands with macrocyclic unit dibenzo-18-crown-6, their Ru(II) complexes, photophysical and electrochemical properties of these complexes.

Outline of the synthesis of the three novel ligands and corresponding complexes is presented in Scheme 1 . BL 1 and BL 2 formed by the reaction of 4,5-diazafluoren-9-one [4] with cis -diaminodibenzo-18-crown-6 and trans -diaminodibenzo-18-crown-6 [5] in ethanol respectively. BL 3 was prepared by the condensation of 1,10-phenanthroline-5,6-dione [6] with tetraaminodibenzo-18-crown-6 [7] in methanol under nitrogen atmosphere [15] . Reaction of Ru(bpy) 2 Cl 2 · 2H 2 O [8] with ligands BL 1–3 respectively under nitrogen atmosphere afforded three bimetallic complexes [(bpy) 2 RuBL 1−3 Ru(bpy) 2 ](PF 6 ) 4 [16] . For each of the three complexes, two diastereoisomers were formed as a result of the chirality of the octahedral coordination around the Ru(II) center. No efforts were made to separate these isomers.

Absorption spectra of complexes 1 , 2 and 3 are shown in Fig. 1 , and the data are summarized in Table 1 . Assignments of the absorption bands are made on the basis of the well-documented optical transitions of other Ru(II) polypyridyl complexes [9] . Absorption spectra of complexes 1 and 2 comprise three distinct region, bands at 285 and 237 nm are attributed to the intraligand π → π ∗ transitions centered on the 2,2′-bipyridine. The set at 445 nm can be assigned as MLCT, dπ → π ∗ transitions. The MLCT absorption maximum is blue-shifted 6 nm comparing with Ru ( bpy ) 3 2 + , which shows the donor property of the BL 1–2 is better than 2,2′-bipyridine.

As observed for [(bpy) 2 Ru(dppz)] 2+ [10] and [Ru(bpy) 2 tpphzRu(bpy) 2 ] 4+ [11] , complex 3 displays a strong MLCT band at 453 nm attributed to the overlapping of dπ(Ru) → π ∗ (bpy) and dπ(Ru) → π ∗ (BL 3 ), and one characteristic π(BL 3 ) → π ∗ (BL 3 ) band at 393 nm. The strong bands below 300 nm can be attributed to π(bpy) → π ∗ (bpy).

Cyclic voltammetry of the three complexes are measured in CH 3 CN, and data are summarized in Table 1 . They all show one Ru(II)-centered oxidation and three ligand-centered reductions.

Oxidation of the complexes involves removal of an electron from dπ orbital of Ru(II). Cyclic voltammetry of complex 1 exhibit a Ru(II)-centered oxidation at 1.32 V. This potential is slightly more positive (by about 40 mV) than Ru ( bpy ) 3 2 + due to the higher π acidity of the bridging ligand BL 1 , but compared with parent complex [(bpy) 2 Ru(dafo)](PF 6 ) 2 [12] , this potential is slightly more negative (by about 70 mV), which indicates that BL 1 is less acidic than dafo. In the current study, bimetallic complex 1 shows a single, unperturbed oxidation wave in cyclic voltammetry and a single peak without broadening in differential pulse voltammetry. On the basis of the above results, the oxidation can be ascribed to a two-electron reversible process.

The reduction at around −0.86 V is a two-electron reversible process associated with the BL 1 . This is consistent with the addition of electron on the low energy LUMO mainly localized on BL 1 to give complex [(bpy) 2 RuBL 2− Ru(bpy) 2 ] 2+ . The second reduction at −1.45 V is quasi-reversible and located on one of the two bpy ligands on each metallic terminal, adding two electrons in the bpy type LUMO + 1 to give the species [(bpy)(bpy ·− )RuBL 2− Ru(bpy ·− )(bpy)]. Just as the oxidation, the two one-electron reductions of the remote bpy appear at the same potential, indicating that the two sites are not interacting. The third reduction appearing at −1.76 V is irreversible and yields the species [(bpy − )(bpy − )RuBL 2− Ru(bpy − )(bpy − )] 2− . Electrochemistry behavior of complex 2 is the same as 1 .

Redox properties of complex 3 are similar to that of (bpy) 2 Ru(dppz) 2+ , the oxidation potential is very close to that of Ru ( bpy ) 3 2 + , appearing little influence of the strong π-accepting character of BL 3 . This is consistent with the fact that the HOMO has little contribution from the phenazine part [10] and [11] . In contrast with the reversible oxidation properties, the first two-electron reduction process shows that complex 3 is better electron acceptor than Ru ( bpy ) 3 2 + by about 0.2 V. The remaining two waves located at −1.39 and −1.74 V consisted of one two-electron processes for each wave related to reduction of the bipyridine ligands on each ruthenium center. The reductions of the bipyridine ligands occurred sequential at each metal center prior to reduction of the second bipyridine ligand. The reduction processes of complex 3 are presented in Scheme 2 .

The emission band maxima and emission quantum yields of Ru(II) complexes are summarized in Table 2 . Upon excitation in the MLCT band of the complexes, 1 and 2 are non-emissive at room temperature in CH 3 CN, but they display similar vibrational components to Ru ( bpy ) 3 2 + at 77 K in 4:1 EtOH/MeOH glassy matrix [13] ( Fig. 2 ). BL 1 and BL 2 are better π-donors than 2,2′-bipyridine, which result in a relatively weak ligand field in [(bpy) 2 RuBL 1−2 Ru(bpy) 2 ](PF 6 ) 4 such that low-lying MC states are available to quench the luminescent 3 MLCT state [14] . Complexes 1 and 2 exhibit their characteristic emission at around 572 nm in 4:1 EtOH:MeOH glassy matrix. Contrary to complexes 1 and 2 , complex 3 shows an intense emission both in CH 3 CN at room temperature and in 4:1 ethanol:methanol glassy matrix at 77 K ( Fig. 3 ).

In conclusion, three dinuclear Ru(II) polypyridyl complexes containing dibenzo-18-crown-6 have been synthesized. These complexes exhibit abundant electrochemical and spectroscopic properties. Taking advantage of the character that dibenzo-18-crown-6 can selectively bind K + , they have potential utility as chemosenors for probing K + .
