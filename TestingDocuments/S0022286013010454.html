Novel 2-(naphthalen-1-yl)-5-stilbene-1,3,4-oxadiazole molecules: Synthesis, optical properties and DFT calculation

Abstract

Six novel 1,3,4-oxadiazole derivatives containing naphthalene and stibene units were synthesized by Wittig–Horner reaction in good yield. Their structures were characterized by FT-IR, 1 H NMR, 13 C NMR and elemental analysis. The optical properties were investigated by UV–Vis absorption and fluorescence emission spectra in THF solution. Their optimized structures and the electron density distribution of HOMO and LUMO were performed by density functional theory (DFT) at the (B3LYP)/6-31G * level. The compounds exhibit blue fluorescence emission with the fluorescence quantum yield ranged from 0.05 to 0.59. The effects of substituents on the emission spectra of these compounds are interpreted. The HOMO and LUMO levels of target compounds are −5.86 to −5.64 eV and −2.79 to −2.13 eV, respectively.

1. Introduction

In recent years, heterocycles have attracted much attention for their excellent photophysical properties, outstanding biological and medical activities [1] ; [2] . 1,3,4-Oxadiazoles are an important class of heterocyclic compounds that have a wide range of drugs and biological activities, such as antimicrobial, antifungal, insecticidal, anti-inflammatory, antihypertensive, analgesic, hypoglycemic and antitubercular [3] ; [4] ; [5] ; [6] ; [7] ; [8] ; [9] . In addition, they have been widely used as organic electronic materials due to their high durability, thermal stability, easily polarization and good optical properties [10] ; [11] ; [12] ; [13] . The widespread use of 1,3,4-oxadiazoles as a scaffold in biological and physical chemistry establishes this unit as an important structure. Stilbenes have numerous implications in plant disease resistance and human health. They are well known for broad spectrum of biological activities, including antioxidants, antibacterial, antifungal and insecticidal [14] ; [15] ; [16] ; [17] ; [18] ; [19] . Moreover, stilbenyl two-photon materials which demonstrate distinctive blue emission properties become the current research focus [20] ; [21] ; [22] . The different functional substituents introduced to the stilbene can changed their properties. Naphthalene unit is an attract fluorophore with a variety of biological effects, which contain anti-inflammatory, insecticidal and antimycobacterial [23] ; [24] ; [25] . Thus, the 1,3,4-oxadiazole based on stilbene and naphthalene units was regard as an active scaffold in drug and material design.

In this paper, six novel 1,3,4-oxadiazole derivatives bearing naphthalene and stibene units were efficiently synthesized via Wittig–Horner reaction and were characterized by FT-IR, 1 H NMR, 13 C NMR and elemental analysis. UV–Vis absorption and fluorescence emission spectra of the compounds in THF solution were investigated. Their optimized structures and the electron density distribution of HOMO and LUMO were performed by density functional theory (DFT) at the (B3LYP)/6-31G * level.

2. Experimental

2.1. General

Melting points were determined using RY-1 melting point apparatus. 1 H NMR and 13 C NMR spectra were recorded in CDCl 3 on a Bruker AVANCE-400 MHz NMR spectrometer using TMS as an internal standard. FT-IR spectra were measured as KBr pellets on a Bruker TENSOR 27 in the region of 4000–400 cm −1 . Elemental analyzes were performed on a Vario EL cube V2.1.0 elemental analyzer. UV–vis absorption spectra were recorded on a Hitachi UV-3010 spectrophotometer. Fluorescence spectra were obtained on a Hitachi F-4500 spectrophotometer at room temperature. The purity of the compounds was confirmed by TLC on silica gel ‘G’-coated glass plates.

2.2. Synthesis

To a stirred solution of the aromatic aldehyde (1.7 mmol) and the diethyl 4-(5-(naphthalen-1-yl)-1,3,4-oxadiazol-2-yl)benzylphosphornate (0.9 g, 1.7 mmol) in anhydrous N,N-dimethylformamide (15 ml) under nitrogen atmosphere was added dropwise a solution of t-BuOK (2 g, 3%) in ethanol at room temperature. Then the reaction proceeded at 70 °C about 5 h. Then the resulting mixture was filtered and washed with ethanol. The residue was recrystallized from ethanol/DMSO.

3. Results and discussion

3.1. Synthesis and characterization

The target compounds were synthesized through Wittig–Horner reaction. The reaction route for the synthesis and the structures of the target molecules are shown in Scheme 1 . The diethyl 4-(5-(naphthalen-1-yl)-1,3,4-oxadiazol-2-yl)benzylphosphornate react with various aldehydes, via Wittig–Horner reaction, to get a series of stilene-cored chromophores based on 1,3,4-oxadiazole bearing naphthyl unit in good yields(yield >80%). The reaction proceeds smoothly and cleanly under mild conditions. It can be observed that the yield is increased with the increasing of the electron-withdrawing ability of the group at the same position of phenyl, indicated that the strong electron-withdrawing ability is beneficial for the Wittig–Horner reaction. The yield is also related to the group attached at different positions on the benzene ring ( p- > m- > o- ), which can be explained by steric hindrance. The melting points have the similar trend ( p- > m- > o- ).

The structures of a – f were deduced from their 1 H NMR, 13 C NMR and elemental analysis. In the 1 H NMR spectra, a doublet appears at the lowest field ( δ = 9.30) corresponding to one proton of naphthalene. On this basis it has been possible to find the total number of hydrogen atoms in the molecules and the number of hydrogen atoms assigned to each signal and each type of hydrogen atoms, respectively. The 1 H NMR spectrum of b show two fine doublets at δ : 7.14 and 7.10 ppm as well as coupling constant (16.7 and 16.3 Hz) corresponding to the olefinic protons (CH CH) from trans-stilbene. At δ : 8.26–7.93 ppm, double peaks and a triplet could be assigned to the naphthalene. At δ : 7.51, 7.30, 7.38, and 7.27, a singlet, a triplet and two doublets could be assigned to 2-H, 5-H, 4-H and 6-H from the aromatic protons, respectively. At δ : 7.67–7.57, multiplets could be assigned to the overlapped of the protons from naphthalene and aromatic ring (C 6 H 4 ). In the 13 C NMR spectra, there are two peaks at around 164.60 and 164.00 ppm, which is assigned to two C atoms of 1,3,4-oxadiazole. Generally, the number of protons and the chemical shifts are consistent with their construction.

3.2. Geometry optimization

The calculated molecular structure of target compounds and its numbering are shown in Fig. 1 . The optimized structural parameters of compounds calculated at the B3LYP/6-31G * level are listed in Supporting materials in accordance with the atom numbering given in Fig. 1 . It can be seen that molecules a and d have non-planar, which explains that substituent group attached at o- position phenyl increase the steric hindrance. The structure of molecules b , c , e and f are similar and almost planar. Table (Supporting materials) shows that the dihedral angles in a and d between vinyl (C31 C32) and benzene (C35 C36) are 160.80° and 146.57°, respectively. The o -NO 2 group positioned on phenyl has greater effect on the stereochemical conformation than o -Br. It also can be observed that the substituents at 2-position phenyl increased the non-planar of the whole molecule according to their intermolecular dihedral angles. The intermolecular dihedral angles of b , c , e and f are closed to 180° or −180°, meaning that they are almost in the plane. Compared with the theoretical value of the bond length, the bond lengths of C5 C18(1.462 Å), C20 C21(1.454 Å), C28 C31(1.463 Å) and C32 C35 (1.465 Å) are shorter than the length of C C(1.54 Å), indicating that the molecules possess the large conjugation. It also can be seen that the bond lengths of 2-(naphthalen-1-yl)-5-stilbene-1,3,4-oxadiazole scaffold are closed, indicating that the different substituents have a limited effect on their basic skeleton structure.

3.3. Vibrational frequency

All the IR spectra of title compounds are similar due to their similar structure. The experimental IR spectrum and the theoretical IR spectrum at B3LYP/6-31G * level of compound e are shown in Fig. 2 . As shown in Fig. 2 , the calculated vibrational wavenumbers are higher than their experimental values for the major functional groups, which may be ascribe to the environment (gas and solid phase). A broad peak appears at 3440 cm −1 in experimental IR spectra attributed to the influence to the unavoidable ʋ O H peak of water. The observed moderate bands at 1605–1608 cm −1 attributed to C N and strong bands at 1493–1518 cm −1 corresponded to aromatic C C are in good agreement with the calculated wavenumbers at 1624–1632 cm −1 and 1560–1568 cm −1 , respectively. Both experimental IR spectrum and theoretical IR spectrum show a sharp strong band at 1354 cm −1 and 1442 cm −1 , respectively, which is attributed to N O(NO 2 ). Moreover, a moderate sharp band appears at about 963 cm −1 in experimental IR spectra indicating the existence of trans-stilbene [26] .

3.4. Optical properties

The UV–Vis absorption and fluorescence emission of the target compounds in THF solution at 1 × 10 −5 mol/L are summarized in Table 1 . The UV–Vis absorption spectra and fluorescence spectra of the target compounds are given in Fig. 3 ; Fig. 4 , respectively. It can be seen that the maximum absorption wavelengths of a – f are 342, 332, 347, 330, 345 and 355, respectively. Compared with the absorption characteristic of the each target compounds, p- substituted compounds have a little greater towards higher wavelength than other compounds. As can be seen in Fig. 4 , the target compound exhibit blue emission with maximum emission at λ em = 405, 402, 406, 375, 409 and 406, respectively. The fluorescence spectra of a – c show two double maximum peaks caused by different vibrational energy levels [27] . The fluorescence intensity of a – c is stronger than the intensity of d – f as the electron-withdrawing NO 2 group reduces the fluorescence. Compounds a , b and c have the similar maximum emission peaks, indicating that the halogen substituents and the substitution sites have a limited effect on the emission. Compared with e and f , d shows blue shift, which might be the strong steric hindrance destroyed the rigid of molecule. The fluorescence quantum yields Φ x = ( A s × F x × n 2 x × Φ s )/( A x × F s × n 2 s ), where A is the absorbance at the excitation wavelength, F is the area under the fluorescence curve, n is the refraction index. Subscripts s and x refer to the standard and the testing sample of unknown quantum yield, respectively. Quinine sulfate in 0.5 M sulfuric acid was used as fluorescence standard ( Φ = 0.546) [28] . Table 1 shows that the fluorescence quantum yields range from 0.05 to 0.59. It can be observed that the Φ x values of d , e , f are lower than other three compounds, owing to the strong electron-withdrawing NO 2 group which results in the fluorescence quenching.

3.5. Theoretical calculation

The optimized structures and the electron density distribution of title compounds were performed by density functional theory (DFT) at the (B3LYP)/6-31G * level [29] . The frontier molecular orbital profiles of title compounds are shown in Fig. 5 . It can be observed that the HOMOs and LUMOs of a , b , and c are delocalized along the whole molecules. The HOMOs of d , e and f are also almost spread the whole molecules, while the LUMOs are mainly localized on 1,3,4-oxadiazole, stilbene and nitro-substituent, which might be due that strong electron withdrawing group NO 2 attract the charge density toward itself. Thus, it can be presumed that the absorption and the emission are controlled mostly by the 1,3,4-oxadiazole and stilbene units. The theoretical HOMO, LUMO energy levels and band gap of title compounds are −5.86 to −5.64 eV, −2.79 to −2.13 eV and 3.15 to 3.52 eV, respectively. The high HOMO energy levels are beneficial to hole-injection.

4. Conclusion

Six novel 2-(naphthalen-1-yl)-5-stilbene-1,3,4-oxadiazole derivatives were synthesized in good yield via Wittig–Horner reaction under the mild condition. The compounds were characterized by FT-IR, 1 H NMR, 13 C NMR, elemental analysis and their optical properties were investigated by UV–Vis and fluorescence spectra. The analytical data were consistent with their structure. The compounds exhibited strong blue emission with the moderate fluorescence quantum yield (0.05–0.59). Theoretical calculation was performed to obtain the optimized structures and the electron density distribution of frontier orbital. They possess good intramolecular charge transfer ability with high HOMO (−5.86 to −5.64 eV) and low LUMO (−2.79 to −2.13 eV). And the 1,3,4-oxadiazole and stilbene units play an important part in their absorption and emission.
